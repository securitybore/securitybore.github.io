Understanding MPEG

A 5-day industrial short course

This 5-day course explains how MPEG video compression works. We show you how MPEG relates to the way people perceive video, and explain the tools that are used to compress the video content with minimal perceived loss. We also explain how an MPEG bit-stream is encoded, and clarify how the computational requirements are encoded in the bit-stream. The end result is that you will understand how MPEG works and how an MPEG bit-stream is constructed.

You will learn:

  • how people perceive still images and video
  • how MPEG tools selectively discard content to compress data
  • how characteristics of video sequences are exploited for further compression
  • how computational requirements are coded as Levels and Profiles
  • how an MPEG bit-stream is encoded
  • how audio/video synchronization is encoded in the bit-stream

    Practical work is based on simple exercises that help you to see the effect of the different tools and processes, and to look inside MPEG bit-streams. You will:

    • see the effect of selectively discarding data
    • apply color sub-sampling to images
    • apply 2D Discrete Cosine Transform analysis to images
    • apply DCT based compression to images
    • apply motion compensation to video sequences
    • view the contents of MPEG video bit-streams
