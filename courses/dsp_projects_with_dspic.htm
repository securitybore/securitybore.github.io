DSP with DSPic class

A series of five 1-day classes held in Woking, Surrey, England

Project-based 'hands-on' DSP learning

This series of five 1-day classes introduces DSP through 'hands-on' work using the Microchip dsPIC 'DSP microcontroller' as a platform for implementation. Taking an innovative approach based we lead you through all the stages from setting up a development system through designing, implementing, testing and debugging your real-time DSP project. After each 1-day class we set you a homework assignment where you use the DSP hardware to put into practise what you learnt: at the next class we lead you through the next stages and your 'hands-on' project advances. At the end of the course you keep the DSP platform an dthe project you implemented on it - concrete evidence of what you learnt and a platform for you to develop your own ideas. Not just about the hardware, this course teaches you DSP - in all its aspects from theory through design and implementation - in a practical 'hands-on' way that is motivating, exciting and effective.

You will learn how to

  • set up the DSP hardware and its sofware development environment
  • use the DSP hardware to analyze and quantify the signal and noise sources
  • specify, design and apply DSP for specific applications
  • implement the DSP in C language, in real-time, on the DSP hardware platform
  • test and debug your application

Practical work is based on simple but realistic design scenarios in which you will be given a DSP requirement specifiation. You will then:

  • Measure and estimate the signal and noise characteristics
  • Analyze the requirement and refine the specification
  • Decide on the DSP processing that you will apply
  • Estimate the required sample rates and arithmetic precision
  • Specify and design any required filters and FFT analysis based on the signal and noise estimation
  • Implement the required processing in simulation
  • Estimate the computational requirements
  • Implement the processing in real-time on a simple DSP microcontroller
